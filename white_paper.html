Abstract

This white paper defines the formal architectural standards of SecureMessenger, a communication platform engineered for Technical Surrender. By mathematically excluding the developer from the user's data lifecycle, we achieve a state of sovereign privacy. SecureMessenger satisfies the requirements of GDPR Article 11, the German TDDDG, and the Digital Services Act (DSA) by ensuring that no identifiable data is processed, stored, or accessible at any point in the relay chain.

1. Identity & Zero-Knowledge Entry

Identity is founded on BIP39 Mnemonic Entropy, removing the need for traditional user databases.

  • Local Seed Generation: A 12-word recovery phrase is generated via 256-bit local entropy. This seed is the only source of truth and never leaves the device.
  • PBKDF2 Hardening: Passwords and seeds are transformed via client-side PBKDF2 with 10,000 iterations. The server only receives a "Scrambled Fingerprint" for session authentication.
  • HMAC-SHA256 Routing: Human usernames are hashed locally using a 512-bit quantum-hardened salt. This deterministic hashing ensures that routing data is stripped of identifying characteristics upon arrival at the relay.

2. Cryptographic Ratcheting (Signal Protocol)

We utilize the Signal Double Ratchet for all End-to-End Encryption (E2EE), ensuring every message has its own unique, rotating key.

  • Perfect Forward Secrecy (PFS): Encryption keys rotate every 48 hours via Signed PreKey Rotation. Compromising one key cannot decrypt past or future traffic.
  • Automatic Repair Handshakes: If a device detects a session desync (e.g., identity reset), the client automatically triggers an Invisible Handshake to re-negotiate the secure tunnel instantly.
  • PreKey Replenishment: The client monitors and replenishes server-side PreKey batches to maintain "Always-On" E2EE initialization.

3. Persistence & Database Hardening

The device is a vault, not a witness. We implement SQLCipher AES-256 byte-level encryption.

  • Wrapped Key Solution: The master key is encrypted by a hardware-backed key anchored in the phone's Secure Element (TEE/SE), ensuring the key cannot be extracted even with root access.
  • RAM Sanitization: Master keys are handled as ByteArrays and physically zeroed out using Arrays.fill() the millisecond the database handshake completes.
  • Scorched Earth Pre-Check: Upon startup, the app audits the database integrity. If a plaintext header or hash mismatch is detected, the file is physically destroyed before initialization.

4. Relay & Metadata Sterilization

SecureMessenger implements a Blind Relay architecture to prevent metadata harvesting.

  • Metadata Stripping: The relay automatically strips all non-routing metadata, including original filenames and device identifiers. All attachments are stored as anonymized binary blobs.
  • Traffic Analysis Protection: The client implements Synthetic Noise Injection and traffic padding to prevent network-level observers from analyzing transmission frequency or packet sizes.
  • Ephemeral Persistence: All data is subject to a 24-hour Time-To-Live (TTL) index. After 24 hours, binary blobs are physically incinerated from the infrastructure.

5. Deniable Sovereignty (Duress Protocol)

  • The Panic PIN: A secondary user-defined password that, if entered at login, triggers a silent, total forensic wipe of all keys, contacts, and messages.
  • The 503 Alibi: Post-wipe, the app displays a fake "Network Error (503)" to provide a technical excuse for an empty vault during physical inspections.
  • OS Shielding: FLAG_SECURE blocks screenshots, screen recording, and masks content in the Android task switcher.

6. Regulatory & Legal Framework

Built on the legal doctrine of Compliance by Inability. Because we do not possess identities, keys, or logs, we are technically and legally excluded from the ability to fulfill data extraction or content moderation requests.
