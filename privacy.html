Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 13, 2026

Zero-Knowledge Disclosure: SecureMessenger utilizes a "Blind Relay" architecture. This application is developed by an independent solo developer in Europe and is designed so that I have no technical power to identify users, map social graphs, or decrypt communications. My architecture is built on the principle of total surrender of control over user data.

1. Data I Do NOT Collect

To ensure absolute privacy, I do not require, collect, or store:

  • Real names, phone numbers, or email addresses.
  • Plaintext passwords or recovery phrases (BIP39 mnemonics).
  • Browser history, device identifiers (IMEI/Serial), or location data.

2. Anonymized Routing & The Blind Relay

Under the GDPR, I process only the absolute functional minimum necessary to route encrypted data packets:

  • Routing ID (HMAC-SHA256): Your username never leaves your device in plaintext. It is transformed locally into a non-reversible 64-character hash. I only see this hash; I cannot link it back to your human identity.
  • Zero-Knowledge Passwords: Passwords are hashed locally using SHA-256. I only store "Scrambled Fingerprints," making server-side breaches useless.
  • Metadata Scrubbing: My server logs are hardcoded to redact IP addresses and scrub query parameters. I do not track who you are or who you talk to.

3. The Local Encrypted Vault (Ghost Protocol)

Your data is protected on your physical device using forensic-grade safeguards:

  • Encryption-at-Rest (SQLCipher): Your local database is encrypted with AES-256. The master key is generated randomly and stored in your device’s hardware-backed Keystore (TEE/SE).
  • RAM Sanitization: To prevent memory-scraping attacks, all cryptographic keys are handled as byte arrays and are physically zeroed out in RAM immediately after use.
  • Physical Guard: The application prevents screenshots and hides content from the "Recents" menu. The optional Biometric Lock provides a secondary layer of physical security.

4. Active Decay & Forensic Shredding

SecureMessenger does not just "delete" data; it destroys it:

  • Targeted Shredding: When you delete a message or shred history, the app performs a two-stage wipe: it overwrites the content with random data before physical deletion.
  • Local TTL (Self-Destruct): You may choose a Retention Policy (1 Hour, 24 Hours, 30 Days). My "Active Decay" engine wakes up hourly to securely scrub and purge expired data.
  • Database Vacuuming: Following a shredding operation, I trigger a physical VACUUM command to ensure no forensic remnants remain on the disk.

5. Data Retention & Volatility (Server-Side)

Messages are deleted from the relay immediately upon successful delivery. Any undelivered encrypted blobs are automatically purged via a 30-day TTL (Time-To-Live) index. I maintain no permanent records.

6. Zero-Discovery Policy

To prevent userbase enumeration, search is Exact-Match only. You cannot browse the user list. You must possess the exact human username or the 64-character Routing ID to find a contact.

7. Right to Erasure (GDPR)

I provide a "Secure Wipe & Logout" option. This "Nuclear Option" physically destroys all cryptographic keys on the device, clears the local database, and signals the server to destroy any associated Routing ID records, fulfilling your Right to Erasure instantly.

8. Report Abuse (DSA Article 16)

In compliance with the EU Digital Services Act, users may report Routing IDs for illegal activity. I will review reports and may restrict specific Routing IDs from the relay, though I cannot access message content due to the E2EE architecture.
