2. GDPR Article 11 (Processing of Anonymous Data)

In accordance with Art. 11 GDPR, SecureMessenger is architected for total anonymity.

  • No Identification: We do not collect, store, or process names, phone numbers, email addresses, or IP metadata.
  • Blind-Relay Architecture: All routing data is stripped of identifying characteristics upon arrival. We do not maintain logs of "who talked to whom" or "when."
  • Data Subject Rights: Because we are technically unable to identify natural persons, we cannot fulfill "Right to Access" or "Right to Portability" requests. To do so would require the collection of new personal data, which violates our core principle of data minimization (Art. 5 GDPR).

3. Forensic Data Minimization & Metadata Suppression

To satisfy the requirements of Art. 5 GDPR (Integrity and Confidentiality), the following protocols are active:

  • Metadata Sterilization: The service automatically strips all non-routing metadata (e.g., original filenames, device identifiers, and MIME types). All attachments are stored as anonymized, encrypted binary blobs (application/octet-stream).
  • Traffic Analysis Protection: The client implements automated Traffic Padding and Synthetic Noise Injection to prevent network-level observers from analyzing packet sizes or transmission frequency.
  • Ephemeral Persistence (TTL): All relayed data is subject to a 24-hour Time-To-Live (TTL) index. After 24 hours, all binary blobs are physically destroyed from the infrastructure. No backups or archives are maintained.
